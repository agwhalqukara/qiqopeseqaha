    February is coming, and there will be new seasons of some of Amazon’s original series in February and a new thriller series starring some of our favorite actors. We have a good month ahead of us. This past year has seen a proliferation of fantasy dramas like House of the Dragon and The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power. In addition, Season 2 of Carnival Row also premiered on Amazon Prime Video. This show is set in a fantastical world where human and nonhuman races are at odds, and its second and final season will pick up where the first left off, with former inspector Rycroft Philostrate looking into a string of grisly murders.

    Therefore, below are our recommendations for the best February 2023 movies and TV shows. Your Amazon Prime membership already includes access to all of these shows at no additional cost.

    10. The Estate

    The Estate is the story of two sisters. To inherit their wealthy aunt’s estate, the two sisters try to succeed over their terminally ill, unyielding aunt, but they soon discover that the rest of their greedy family has the same goal. Sisters and business partners Macey and Savannah are dealing with the news that their wealthy, distant aunt Hilda has terminal cancer while trying to keep their business afloat. The sisters wasted no time in making the trip to meet with Hilda to win her favor and inherit some of her wealth. When they get there, they discover that several of their cousins had the same idea, and now everyone is trying to win Aunt Hilda’s favor.

    9. Gary and His Demons: Seasons 1-2

    Mark Little and Mark Satterthwaite of Canada’s Mondo Media created the animated sitcom Gary and His Demons for the VRV service. This show centers on a stodgy old demon slayer with nothing to live for. Gary struggles to maintain interest in his Earth-saving duty, which he did not ask for and does not want, because of his burdened “Chosen One” status and the support of a team of specialists he cannot relate to.

    8. Carnival Row: Season 2

    Carnival Row: Season 2 is a crime drama directed by René Echevarria. After the invasion of the exotic homelands by human empires, a growing population of mythological immigrant creatures finds it difficult to coexist with humans. The creatures have been forbidden from living, loving, and flying freely, but hope still exists in the shadows. An increasingly intolerant society doesn’t stop human detective Rycroft “Philo” Philostrate from having an affair with faerie refugee Vignette Stonemoss.

    7. Star Trek: Picard: Season 3

    Akiva Goldsman is the American creator of the Star Trek: Picard television series. In ‘Star Trek: Picard,’ Sir Patrick Stewart plays the role of retired Admiral Jean-Luc Picard 18 years after the happening of ‘Star Trek: Nemesis. The story takes spot at the end of the 24th century. Even as he moves on to the next phase of his life, the death of Brent Spiner’s Lieutenant Commander Data and the destruction of Romulus continue to weigh heavily on him.

    6. Five Feet Apart

    Five Feet Apart is a Romance drama by Justin Baldoni. Stella Grant and Will Newman, both in their teens, have cystic fibrosis (CF), a genetic condition that progressively damages organs and leaves patients vulnerable to infections. Patients with cystic fibrosis should not get any closer than six feet apart because of their weakened immune systems. Stella takes her medication consistently and does everything by the book regarding her treatment plan.

    5. Die Hart

    Season 2 of Kevin Hart’s Roku original comedy “Die Hart” will feature several new cast members. Hart portrayed a fictionalized version of himself who realized his goal of becoming a real-life action hero in the first season of “Die Hart,” in which John Travolta also starred. In Season 2, Hart is working to establish himself as the all-time greatest action star. He has an idea for a ground-breaking film in which the plot twists and turns so rapidly that not even Kevin can predict what will happen next.

    4. The Consultant: Season 1

    Tony Basgallop’s The Consultant is a forthcoming workplace comedy/thriller series adapted from Bentley Little’s novel of the same name. Mr. Patoff, a consultant in a bow tie, appears to take over management of CompWare after a major merger fails.

    3. How To Train Your Dragon 2

    How to Train Your Dragon 2 is based on the book by Cressida Cowell of the same name. It is a sequel to the 2011 computer-animated action fantasy flick How to Train Your Dragon. There has been peace and prosperity in Berk between the dragons and Viking villagers for the past five years. Hiccup explores and maps previously uncharted territory with his dragon Toothless the Night Fury. Hiccup is being pressured by his father, Stoick the Vast, to take over as chieftain now that he is 20, despite his uncertainty about being ready.

    2. Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind

    A romantic science fiction drama, Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind was written by Charlie Kaufman and released in 2004. Clementine Kruczynski, the girlfriend of Joel Barish, finds out that the New York City company Lacuna has erased her memories of him. Saddened by the loss, he makes the same choice. He anticipates this confrontation by recording a tape for Lacuna in which he describes their tumultuous history together.

    1. Pitch Perfect 2

    Elizabeth Banks directed and produced the American musical comedy film Pitch Perfect 2. In 2015, senior Beca Mitchell and three-time super senior Chloe Beale led the Barden Bellas to their second consecutive championship. The Bellas have been crowned ICCA National Champions for three years in a row. The whole story is celebrated in this Comedy/Musical drama.
