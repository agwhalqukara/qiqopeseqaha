    11 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Leon Wadham, a 22-year-old exceptionally outstanding young actor, will portray Kemen, Pharazôn’s son, in the forthcoming fantasy-drama series The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power.

    The official summary for the fantasy drama series on Amazon Prime Video is as follows:

    “Beginning in a period of relative peace, the series follows an ensemble cast of old and new characters as they face the long-feared return of evil to Middle-earth.” These countries and personalities will carve out legacies that will live on long after they are gone, from the deepest depths of the Misty Mountains to the gorgeous woodlands of the elf-capital of Lindon, to the stunning island kingdom of Numenor, to the furthest ends of the globe.”

    Since Amazon Studios published his first glimpse, fans have been anxious to discover more about the 22-year-old actor. So, without further ado, let’s get to know Leon Wadham before the series premieres on Amazon Prime Video on Friday, September 1, 2022.

    Leon discusses his character in The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power.

    Leon Wadham described his character Kemen’s connection with his father Pharazôn in The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power in an interview with Collider. He stated:

    Listen, Pharazôn is a wonderful guy. You may say that the offspring of prominent individuals fall into two groups. They may either endeavor to equal and outperform their parents, or they might coast on a sense of entitlement. And I believe he falls into that group at the time we meet…” “…He’s arrived in a position of enormous affluence, you know.” He’s living in the golden era, with everything he’s ever desired. There is no genuine wish for that to go.”

    Leon Wadham went on to say:

    “I believe his father’s displeasure with him stems from the fact that he isn’t bringing any fresh ideas to the table, since the concepts of the day have served him very well personally.” And his trip will require him to question what he stands for, what he believes is worth fighting for, and what that struggle could truly entail.”

    Before The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power airs on Prime Video, learn more about the actor Leon Wadham and his career. Leon Wadham is a 22-year-old writer, theater artist, director, and actor. Toi Whakaari: New Zealand Drama School was where he acquired his training. Lord of the Flies at Auckland Theater Company, Tribes at Silo, The Cape at Circa, The Pitchfork Disney at Q Theater, Tinderbox at BATS, and Essays in Love at Basement Theater are among the many pieces in which the young actor has appeared.

    Aside from famous stage performances, the actor has also acted in a number of important movies and short films, including Weirdoes, Why Does Love? Pirates of the Airwaves, Blankets, Shopping, A Bend on the Road, Monkeys Don’t Mix, The Catch, Under the Mountain, and Jack & Jill are among the films included. In addition to The Bad Seed, Power Rangers Beast Morphers, The Brokenwood Mysteries, Terry Teo, When We Go to War, Go Girls, and The Dark Knight, Leon Wadham has featured in a number of well-known TV dramas and miniseries. The young actor is most recognized for his roles in Go Girls as Levi Hirsh and The Bad Seed as Matt Logan.

    Wadham has a few significant writing credits in addition to his performing credentials. Sunny Skies, a popular TV3 comedy, is on the list. He’s also directed films including School Night, Moving, Golden Boy Series 2, Citizen’s Handbook, and others. The first two episodes of The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power, an absorbing epic series, will launch on Prime Video on Friday, September 2, 2022. The series was established by Patrick McKay and J. D. Payne after being inspired by J. R. R. Tolkien’s cherished work The Lord of the Rings and Appendices.
