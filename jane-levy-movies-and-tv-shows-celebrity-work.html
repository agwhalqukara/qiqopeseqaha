    Early Life and Career Beginnings

    Jane Levy was born on December 29, 1989, in Los Angeles, California. She attended Sir Francis Drake High School in San Anselmo, where she pursued her passion for both hip hop dancing and soccer. During her high school years, she also participated in community theater productions, showcasing her talent and love for acting.

    After graduating, Levy briefly attended Goucher College before enrolling at the Stella Adler Studio of Acting in New York City to further refine her skills. At Stella Adler, she received comprehensive training in various acting techniques, including the Meisner technique and method acting. This educational experience played a crucial role in shaping Levy’s approach to her craft and solidifying her dedication to pursuing a career in acting.

    With her natural talent and the foundational skills acquired through her education, Jane Levy embarked on her professional acting journey. She started auditioning for various roles in films and television shows, determined to make a name for herself in the industry. Her perseverance and commitment soon paid off, as she began to land her first acting gigs and gain recognition for her exceptional talent.

    Breaking Into the Industry

    Equipped with her talent and training, Jane Levy began auditioning for acting roles, determined to break into the industry.     Breakthrough Role on “Suburgatory”

    In 2011, Jane Levy landed her breakthrough role as Tessa Altman in the ABC sitcom “Suburgatory.” Starring alongside Jeremy Sisto and Cheryl Hines, Levy portrayed a sharp-witted and relatable teenager navigating life in the suburbs. Her performance as Tessa received critical acclaim, showcasing her comedic timing and versatility as an actress. The success of “Suburgatory” propelled Levy into the spotlight and opened doors for future opportunities.

    Levy’s portrayal of Tessa Altman in “Suburgatory” allowed her to showcase her talent for comedy in a lead role. Her character’s quick wit and relatability resonated with audiences, making her a fan favorite. The successful run of the show further solidified Levy’s reputation as a talented actress with incredible comedic timing.

    “Suburgatory” not only showcased Levy’s acting skills, but it also allowed her to work alongside other talented actors and comedians. Her chemistry with her co-stars was evident on-screen, contributing to the show’s overall success. Levy’s comedic talents shone through in every episode, earning her recognition and paving the way for future comedic roles in her career.

    Film Career Highlights: From “Evil Dead” to “Don’t Breathe”

    Jane Levy’s film career has been marked by several notable projects, particularly in the horror genre. In 2013, she starred in the remake of the horror classic “Evil Dead,” delivering a captivating performance as Mia, a character struggling with addiction. The film received widespread acclaim, solidifying Levy’s reputation as a talented actress in the genre.

    She continued to showcase her versatility in independent films like “About Alex” and “Bang Bang Baby.” Another standout role was in the critically acclaimed thriller “Don’t Breathe” directed by Fede Álvarez, where Levy played the role of Rocky, a young woman caught in a terrifying home invasion. Her performances in these films further established her as a rising star.

    “I think because I’m naturally kind of a silly person and pretty easygoing, I don’t know if people see me in that way [as a horror actress], but I enjoy making these films,” Levy once said, highlighting her enthusiasm for exploring the genre.

    Television Series Appearances: From “Shameless” to “Castle Rock”

    Recent Projects and Awards Recognition

    Jane Levy’s talent and dedication have propelled her career to new heights, with a string of recent projects that have garnered both critical acclaim and audience appreciation. Her exceptional work in these projects has also earned her recognition in the form of prestigious awards and nominations.

    One of Levy’s notable recent projects is her starring role as Zoey Clarke in the NBC musical comedy-drama series “Zoey’s Extraordinary Playlist.” Her portrayal of the titular character, who discovers her ability to hear people’s inner thoughts through musical numbers, has captivated audiences and critics alike. Levy’s performance in the series earned her a well-deserved Golden Globe nomination for Best Actress in a Television Series Musical or Comedy.

    “Playing Zoey Clarke in ‘Zoey’s Extraordinary Playlist’ has been an incredible experience. The show combines my love for acting and music, allowing me to explore the emotional depth of the character through song. It’s an honor to be recognized with a Golden Globe nomination and to be part of a series that resonates with so many people.” – Jane Levy

    In addition to “Zoey’s Extraordinary Playlist,” Levy has also appeared in other diverse and engaging projects. She starred in the indie film “The Pretenders,” directed by James Franco, which premiered at the 2018 Torino Film Festival. Levy’s portrayal of Catherine, a young woman caught in a love triangle, showcased her versatility as an actress.
